Let’s get started!

The calculation process:

  • Estimate the volume of asphalt needed: Use geometric formulas and project plans or measurements, including width, length, and depth (thickness).
  • Estimate the density of the hotmix to be used: The standard density for asphalt is 145 lb/ft3 (2322 kg/m3).
  • Multiply the volume by the density (in the same units) to get the weight.

The calculator formula applied in Asphalt Calculator:

A simple formula is applied to Asphalt Calculator for estimate the asphalt volume and weight required: Volume (cubic feet) = Area (length × width) × Thickness Weight = Volume × Asphalt Density (145 lbs per cubic foot)

Inputs You’ll Need:

  • Area Dimensions: The length and width of the project area (in feet or meters).
  • Thickness: The desired depth of asphalt (in inches or centimeters).
  • Asphalt Density: Typically 145 lbs/ft³ or 2,320 kg/m³.

Asphalt basics & applications

Asphalt is a black, sticky, and highly viscous liquid or semi-solid form of petroleum. It is found naturally, but more commonly results from refinement and is classified as a pitch. Asphalt is highly recyclable, offering both cost savings and environmental benefits.

Asphalt (tarmac) is widely used for road surfaces, including highways, inner-city and inter-city roads, local roads, car parks, driveways, and pavements. These are the primary reasons people use our calculator. It's estimated that around 94% of the 2.6 million paved roads in the U.S. are made from asphalt. You can also find asphalt on racetracks, tennis courts, and dams. Given its water resistant nature, it’s ideal for cable and pipe coatings and general waterproofing. Asphalt is used in bituminous waterproofing products, where it's a component of roofing felt and is used for sealing flat roofs. Bitumen has other valuable qualities, including durability, high traction, and low light reflectivity.

In road construction, asphalt typically serves as a binding agent in asphalt concrete production. Bitumen is mixed with fine and coarse aggregates, such as sand, gravel, and crushed rock. Recycled polymers (e.g., recycled rubber tires) may be added to the hot mix asphalt to modify its properties depending on the intended application.

Types of asphalt

There are more than one type of asphalt, primarily differentiated by the mixing temperature. Selecting the right type is crucial, and calculating its requirement basis the density more important.

  • Hot Mix Asphalt (HMA)
  • Warm Mix Asphalt
  • Driveway Mix Asphalt
  • Porous Asphalt
Type Description
Hot Mix Asphalt (HMA) Also known as "Dense-Graded Mix," this is the most widely used asphalt type, ideal for all traffic conditions, road surfacing, and repairs. HMA offers excellent friction and durability. The binder is heated at high temperatures to remove moisture and ensure a proper mix. This type closely matches the asphalt density used in most calculators.
Warm Mix Asphalt (WMA) Used in approximately one-third of road-paving projects, WMA is produced at lower temperatures than HMA. It is ideal for paving in off-season months, during nighttime projects, and in environmentally sensitive areas due to reduced emissions and energy consumption.
Driveway Mix Asphalt A budget-friendly option tailored for driveways and parking lots. This mix consists of crushed stone, sand, and gravel, offering a solid yet economical surface. While not as durable as HMA, it is sufficient for low-traffic areas.
Porous Asphalt Designed for superior water drainage, this mix consists mainly of crushed stone with minimal sand content, making it highly permeable. It eliminates the need for additional drainage systems. Though more expensive, it is environmentally friendly and helps prevent water pooling and runoff issues.
